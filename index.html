SO2 Concentration Monitoring Website

This website was created as a public platform to see how the concentration of SO2 due to volcanic eruptions in the 2019-2022 period

TROPOMI Sentinel-5P

The spectral indices used in this project are the Sentinel-5P TROPOMI satellite imagery. Sentinel-5P TROPOMI is a type of passive satellite that principally measures the Top Of Atmosphere (TOA), solar radiation reflected and emitted from the earth, and launched by the ESA.

Sulphur Dioxide

Sulphur Dioxide (SO2) is a colorless gas with a pungent odor and has a boiling point of -10 degrees. This substance is heavier than air and is toxic if inhaled and can irritate the eyes. SO2 dissolves in water very easily (National Center for Biotechnology Information, 2022).

Based on data from MAGMA PVMBG, Badan Geologi Kementerian ESDM, There are about ten volcanoes that erupt in Indonesia. In fact, some of them are still active today. The distribution of SO2 carried by volcanic ash is important to know as a sign of disaster-prone areas that need to be avoided. Therefore, a public platform was created as a means of monitoring the impact of volcanic eruptions in Indonesia, especially on the ten active volcanoes to date, namely Mount Sinabung, Kerinci, Anak Krakatau, Tangkuban Prahu, Merapi, Bromo, Semeru, Raung, Agung, and Mount Ibu

Google Earth Engine Apps

The application is used to monitor the SO2 concentration through the help of Google Earth Engine Apps. Monitoring is conducted when the volcano erupts and is classified according to the danger scale.

The Google Earth Engine application was created to display a visualization of the distribution of SO2 concentrations that is adjusted for disaster-prone areas, affected areas, and coverage areas every 3km, 5km, and 7km.
